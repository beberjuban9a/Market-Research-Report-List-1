<p><p>The cannabis food and beverage market can be segmented into three types: hemp-derived, marijuana-derived, and synthetic. Hemp-derived products use low-THC strains, offering health benefits and non-psychoactive properties, making them popular in wellness items and snacks. Marijuana-derived products contain higher THC levels, delivering psychoactive effects mainly in edibles and drinks intended for recreational use. Synthetic cannabis, on the other hand, involves lab-created compounds that mimic natural cannabinoids, providing more controlled effects and consistency in formulations. These types cater to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
