<p><p>Heterojunctions with intrinsic thin layers (HIT) are semiconductor devices that incorporate intrinsic layers between p-type and n-type materials. The p-type market features semiconductors that have been doped with acceptors to create an abundance of holes, enhancing hole mobility. Conversely, the n-type market involves semiconductors doped with donors, which contribute excess electrons, improving electron mobility. These structures are crucial for solar cells, offering high efficiency and reduced recombination losses, crucial for advancing photovoltaic technology and driving market growth.</p></p>
